Overview

This custom HAT (Hardware Attached on Top) was designed to sit directly on an NVIDIA Jetson Orin and serve as the central communication hub for the SC Robotics Mars rover. The board bridges the Jetson's high-level processing with the rover's drive, science, and vision subsystems through multiple serial protocols running simultaneously.

Designed entirely in Altium Designer, it was one of the most complex boards I've laid out — requiring careful signal integrity consideration, proper power sequencing, and protection circuitry to survive the demanding conditions of field deployment at the University Rover Challenge.

Communication Protocols

Drive subsystem motor control
Onboard sensor communication
Serial debug and peripheral links
High-speed peripheral interface

Protection & Power

  • Reverse voltage protection to guard against incorrect power connection in the field.
  • Discrete ICs for signal conditioning and level shifting between subsystems.
  • Power distribution designed for the rover's field power supply requirements.

Design Challenges

Routing multiple high-speed serial protocols on a single board requires careful attention to trace impedance, ground plane continuity, and avoiding crosstalk between adjacent signal lines. Managing all of this while keeping the form factor constrained to the Jetson's footprint was the central layout challenge.
